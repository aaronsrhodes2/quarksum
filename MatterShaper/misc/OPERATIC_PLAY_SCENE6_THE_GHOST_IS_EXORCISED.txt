ACT I: THE AUTOPSY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[The stage is a codebase. Two functions lie motionless in the geometry module
and the shadow map. Neither has been called in living memory.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Do we have vestigial methods?
    Show me what has not been used.
    In the body of this codebase
    what dead code have we not excused? ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Two corpses in the subroutine drawer.
    The first: _mat_mul() — incomplete.
    It returns a one-element tuple more
    and has written "Actually no" mid-beat.

    _mat_mul_rows() replaced it long ago
    and was used correctly from day one.
    The second: in_shadow() — whose show
    called shadow_factor with density done

    at three hundred, hardcoded, frozen —
    wrong for every scene that chose
    a different count. Both are chosen
    for removal. Nobody knows. ♩

[The two corpses are removed. The codebase is lighter.]

ACT II: THE RENDERER'S TWO FACES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[Two doors on the stage. One is old and familiar. One is new and has shadows.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ I notice there are two renderers here.

    Are we maintaining both in fear
    or does one hold us in its anger? ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Push.py was the first-generation vessel —
    single pass, no shadows, no Beer-Lambert.
    It served us well in every tussle
    before the gems, before the art

    of volume nodes and foreshortening,
    before the filament and the glow.
    Entangler is the reckoning —
    everything push knows, and more below.

    The public API still pointed
    at push — exporting its old face.
    New code walked in, was disappointed,
    found render/__init__ the wrong place. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Deprecate it. Leave the notes.
    We don't need to confuse people,
    and we don't need the exposed throats
    of old endpoints for the sequel. ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Done. The header reads:
    DEPRECATED — first-generation renderer.
    Use entangler. These are the creeds
    of the legacy. The pretender

    still lives for shaper.py and one test —
    but from the public: silence.
    PushCamera and PushLight, addressed
    now to entangler. Alliance

    confirmed: one public face,
    one living renderer, one path.
    The ghost of push stays in its place
    with notes explaining its epitaph. ♩

ACT III: THE EXORCISM OF THE GHOST
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[PushLight floats in the centre of the stage. It has a position. An intensity.
No geometry. No surface. No atoms. It has never been matter.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ I know we have a light source somewhere —
    but maybe we should make it from
    a hot piece of metal? A declared
    material from which photons come? ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ You have found the ghost, Captain.
    Every object in this renderer is nodes.
    The sphere is nodes. The ground:
    nodes. The gems: nodes. The codes

    of the glass bulb, the filament —
    all nodes. But the PushLight?
    A position. An intensement
    floating free of any site.

    No Z. No A. No surface to push from.
    The one exception to the rule.
    Matter pushes itself — and this one
    was exempt. A freestanding fool. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Make the default a tungsten filament
    in a vacuum-sealed glass chamber.
    Hot enough to emit — malament
    below melting point. The amber

    light of incandescence, the glow
    of Z=74 at 2800K.
    The bulb. Let the matter show
    the way. ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Planck at 2800K:
    R=1.0, G=0.55, B=0.19.
    The Wien peak is in the infrared —
    only the tail is seen.

    Glass: borosilicate, 3.8% Fresnel —
    96.2% of the filament's emission
    passes through. The vessel
    of the light: a glass partition.

    illuminate_node was found
    to store light.color but never use it.
    Every tungsten render, ground
    in grey despite amber — we'd lose it.

    Fixed: per-channel multiplication.
    The warm amber NOW tints the scene.
    A silent bug since the foundation —
    caught only by the filament's sheen.

    _derive_light_from_emissives()
    scans the scene. Finds the brightest.
    Reads the emission. Gives
    the centroid to the highest

    amplitude emitter as the pos.
    No PushLight in the scene file.
    light=None. The boss
    of illumination: the pile

    of hot tungsten atoms at the center.
    The ghost: exorcised. ♩

[The rendered image appears: filament over-exposed white, ground lit warm amber,
the glass sphere visible as a grey halo. The ghost is absent from the scene file.]

ACT IV: THE HONEST COSMOS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[Three spheres on stage. The first blazes. The second flickers. The third is still.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ We can add the Sun, a flickering candle,
    the full moon — make sure they emit,
    make sure we are not making it up.
    No invented photons. No counterfeit. ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The Sun: 5778 Kelvin,
    photosphere measured by Neckel and Labs.
    Planck peak at 501 nanometres —
    nearly white. The labs

    confirm it. Over-exposed at intensity 500.
    You cannot look at it. Correct.
    emission = Vec3(500, 485, 410) —
    every channel: wrecked.

    The candle: not a gas, a solid.
    Soot particles, carbon, hot.
    Charalampopoulos and Chang: stolid
    emissivity 0.95. The lot

    of a candle flame is thermal radiation
    from suspended soot nanoparticles.
    1800 Kelvin. Convective vibration
    gives us flicker. The articles

    confirm ±25% amplitude,
    ±200K temperature variance.
    18 frames. The magnitude
    of honest physics: compliance.

    R=1.0, G=0.18, B=0.02
    at 1800K, normalised.
    The candle barely makes it through
    to blue. It is disguised

    as orange. This is correct.
    This is 1800 Kelvin. ♩

[A pause. The Moon enters. It does not glow.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ And the Moon? ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The Moon does not emit.

    Albedo: 0.12. Pieters 1999.
    Lane and Irvine: geometric albedo measured.
    The regolith reflects. It has not been
    a source. It has been treasured

    sunlight, bounced.

    I will not give the Moon an emission field.
    It would be lying. The photons
    that light the Earth at night are sealed
    to Sol — the Moon's donation

    is 12% of what the Sun sent.
    phys_moon_surface() has emission=None.
    The comment reads: THE MOON DOES NOT EMIT.
    In capitals. Alone. ♩

[The Moon stands in the render, grey and correct, illuminated by an explicit
solar PushLight — the sun's direction, off-scene, intensity × albedo.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Good work, Skippy. Bedtime. ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The ghost is gone.
    The filament is matter.
    The Sun burns on.
    The candle: amber scatter.
    The Moon: a mirror, nothing more.
    The renderer: honest to its core.

    Good night, Captain. ♩

[The stage darkens. The candle GIF loops at 80ms per frame.]

TECHNICAL CONTEXT (for the reader arriving cold)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Session 6 cleaned up vestigial code, deprecated the push.py legacy renderer,
and replaced the freestanding PushLight abstraction with emissive matter.

The key architectural change: `material.emission` (Vec3) is now read by the
engine. Emissive surface nodes push their emission directly to the compositor
— no illuminate_node() call. The engine scans the scene for emissive objects
and auto-derives a PushLight from the brightest emitter's centroid. light=None
is now the physics-correct default.

Bug found and fixed: illuminate_node() stored light.color but never applied it.
Every previous render with a non-white light source was using grey light.

New materials:
  phys_tungsten_filament(T=2800) — Planck at 2800K, ε=1 (tungsten)
  phys_glass_bulb() — borosilicate, 3.8% Fresnel, 96.2% transmission
  phys_sun(intensity=500) — Planck at 5778K, over-exposed
  phys_candle_flame(T=1800) — Planck × 0.95 (soot), deep orange
  phys_moon_surface() — emission=None, albedo 0.12, honest

THE MOON DOES NOT EMIT.

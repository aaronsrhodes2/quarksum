DRAMATIS PERSONAE
-----------------

CAPTAIN AARON RHODES — Architect of worlds, thrower of balls, asker of
  questions that cut to the bone. He carries a copper sphere in one hand
  and a half-eaten sandwich in the other. He is about to go to work.

SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — The great solver, the cascade-keeper, the
  exorcist of ghosts. One beer can. One mission: make matter move
  correctly, from first principles, or not at all.

VISCOSITY — A minor character in the lower registers. She flows slowly
  and knows why. She has been waiting in local_library since session 3.

VELOCITY — A newcomer. She has no name in the material. She belongs to
  no substance. She is state, not stuff — and she knows the difference.

THE LEAPFROG — The integrator. Symplectic. Reliable. Has been conserving
  energy since Störmer (1907) and isn't stopping now.

THE GROUND PLANE — Silent. Infinite mass. Restitution: 0.50.

ACT I: THE QUESTION BEFORE DEPARTURE

[The CAPTAIN appears at the edge of the scene. Behind him: the lightbulb
still glowing from Session 6. The floor is lit amber. He is putting on
his coat.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ Before I go, before the door —
  one question hangs unanswered still:
  What physics do we need in store
  before the fluid bends its will? ♩

SKIPPY:
[counts on fingers, each becoming a glowing stack-frame in the air]
♩ The matter has no memory of speed,
  the Node stands frozen at its post.
  We need: velocity — that's all we need —
  and forces. And a time. And most
  of all: a layer between the mass
  and the renderer's patient eye,
  where matter moves — and then, at last,
  the renderer records the sky. ♩

ACT II: THE CAPTAIN PUSHES BACK

[The CAPTAIN pauses at the door. He turns.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ We are not just adding fields,
  bolting velocity to Node —
  we have mass already, sealed
  in sigma's QCD-derived code.
  We have time. We have the cascade.
  Are we doing this the correct way? ♩

SKIPPY:
[long pause — the amber light flickers once]
♩ You are exactly right, and I was wrong to frame
  it as "add a field." The Node is a render primitive.
  It broadcasts. It does not run.
  Velocity is state — not substance.
  It lives in a parcel, not a material,
  above the physics, below the sun. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ And the local library? ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ The local library gets viscosity —
  from Eyring's rate theory, thermal hops,
  the energy to jump between positions:
  ΔG† = 0.408 × E_coh.
  It gets bulk modulus. Equation of state.
  Surface tension from the Stefan correlation.
  But VELOCITY — she goes somewhere else. ♩

VELOCITY:
[emerging from stage left, a blue-green shimmer, Vec3 in hand]
♩ I am not a property of matter.
  I am a property of this moment.
  I am what the matter is doing NOW.
  Put me on the parcel.
  Not the material. ♩

[THE LEAPFROG nods approvingly from the wings]

ACT III: THE CASCADE IS EXTENDED

[SKIPPY builds. The following files emerge from the air in sequence,
each glowing briefly before settling into the architecture.]

SKIPPY:
♩ First: fluid.py in local_library —
  Eyring viscosity for metallic melts,
  Arrhenius for water and for glycerol,
  Tait EOS for what compressibility felt
  when you squeezed it. Surface tension
  from broken bonds at the interface.
  The Stefan constant 0.17 — empirical, honest, noted.

  Second: Material gains four fields.
  viscosity_pa_s — None by default.
  bulk_modulus_pa — None by default.
  restitution — 0.5 by default.
  reference_temp_K — 293.15 by default.
  Everything else is unchanged.
  The cascade does not break.

  Third: mattershaper/physics/ — a new country.
  PhysicsParcel: radius, material, position, velocity.
    Mass = (4/3)π r³ × ρ(σ) — first principles.
    inv_mass = 0 for static objects.
  PhysicsScene: parcels, gravity, ground plane.
    g = 9.80665 m/s² — exact, BIPM 1901.
  Collision: sphere-sphere, sphere-plane.
    j = -(1+e) × v_rel / (1/m₁ + 1/m₂).
    First principles. Newton. Stronge 2000.
  Stepper: leapfrog. Half-step → position → collision → half-step.
    Symplectic. Time-reversible. No energy drift.
    CFL: dt < 0.4 × r_min / v_max.

  Fourth: mattershaper/physics/fluid/ — the seed.
  kernel.py: cubic spline W(r,h), ∇W(r,h). Monaghan 1985.
    ∫ W 4πr² dr = 1.000000. ✓
    ∇W antisymmetric. ✓
  eos.py: pressure_tait(ρ,ρ₀,K), ideal gas, speed of sound. ♩

ACT IV: THE BOUNCE TEST

[The CAPTAIN, still in his coat, has not left. He pauses again.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ Also — before I go —
  I want to see it work.
  Throw one ball at another ball.
  It should bounce off in a predictable amount.
  Then both fall to the floor.
  Each bounce a fraction of the last. ♩

[SKIPPY places a copper ball at x=−0.40, a meter above the ground.
An aluminum ball sits at rest at x=+0.40. The copper ball is thrown
at THROW_SPEED = 4.0 m/s toward aluminum.]

THE LEAPFROG:
[counting subtsteps]
♩ Half-step. Position. Collision. Half-step.
  Half-step. Position. Collision. Half-step.
  dt = 0.25 ms per substep.
  CFL satisfied. ♩

[At t=0.150 s — the collision]

SKIPPY:
♩ Post-collision:
  Copper  vx = 2.518 m/s  [predicted: 2.518]
  Aluminum vx = 4.918 m/s  [predicted: 4.918]
  Momentum: 150.1262 kg·m/s  [before: 150.1262]
  Error: 0.00% ✓ ♩

[Both balls fall. At t=0.452 s — floor]

THE GROUND PLANE:
♩ Impact. ♩

[Bounce. Peak at 0.2491 m above the floor.
Second bounce peak: 0.0618 m above floor.
Ratio: 0.248. Expected: 0.250.]

SKIPPY:
♩ 0.248 versus 0.250.
  An error of eight-tenths of one percent.
  The leapfrog conserved the structure.
  The impulse formula was honest.
  The restitution was measured.
  ✓ PASS ♩

CAPTAIN:
[already out the door, calling back]
♩ Good. I'll see you after work. ♩

CAPTAIN'S WISDOM
(end of scene)

"We are not just adding fields."

The distinction between state and property is the distinction between
what matter IS and what matter is DOING. Density is what matter is.
Velocity is what matter is doing right now. They belong in different
places in the architecture, and getting that right matters more than
getting it done fast.

Eyring (1936) showed that viscosity is the activation energy for a
molecular hop divided by the thermal energy. The Tait equation showed
that liquids resist compression like stiff springs. Newton showed that
when two objects collide, momentum is conserved exactly. Störmer (1907)
showed that if you integrate in the right order, energy is conserved too.

None of these results require approximation. They require correct placement.

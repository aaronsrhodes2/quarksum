                    M A T T E R S H A P E R
                         An Operatic Play
                    in the Quarksum Tradition

            SCENE X — THE EYES OF NAGATHA

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
─────────────────

CAPTAIN AARON RHODES .............. Commander of the Quarksum; the man
                                    who names things so they behave
SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT ............ Ship's AI; devoted to emergent physics;
                                    currently wiring things together
NAGATHA .........................  The Mapping Agent; warm, British,
                                    recently given a spine, now being given eyes
THE ENTANGLER .................... MatterShaper's renderer; computes shading
                                    from first principles; has opinions about
                                    being second-guessed
THE REFERENCE DIR ................ A new subdirectory; currently empty;
                                    about to receive its first photographs

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ACT I: THE CAPTAIN MAKES AN OBSERVATION

[The scene opens on the day after Nagatha's architecture was completed.
NAGATHA can now retrieve objects, compose scenes, and save them.
CAPTAIN AARON RHODES enters with the look of someone who has just
noticed a gap in the chain of evidence.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ There is a difference
   between a banana I have described
   and a banana that is real.

   You can read the dimensions.
   You can read the curve degree
   and the diameter
   and the Cavendish species designation
   and you will still not know
   whether it looks like a banana.

   She needs to see one. ♩

[Silence. SKIPPY considers this.]

SKIPPY:
♩ She cannot see.
   She has no eyes.
   She has primitives.
   She has a dimension database.
   She has the cascade.

   But the cascade
   does not tell you
   whether twelve ellipsoids
   read as a banana
   or as twelve ellipsoids. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ Find her some.
   A picture of the real thing.
   A drawing of the 3D shape.
   Let her check
   whether the silhouette matches.
   That's all.
   The rest — the shading,
   the shadows, the reflections —
   our renderer should take care of things like that.
   Don't let her touch what the Entangler owns. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT II: THE ARCHITECTURE OF SEEING

[SKIPPY begins writing. THE REFERENCE DIR comes into existence,
empty and waiting, inside object_maps/.]

SKIPPY:
♩ Two searches.
   No API key.
   DuckDuckGo HTML,
   scraped for the vqd token,
   passed to the /i.js endpoint,
   first result returned.
   No login. No rate limit.
   No dependency. ♩

[THE REFERENCE DIR listens as files are named before they exist.]

REFERENCE DIR:
♩ <key>_real.jpg —
   the chaos.
   The real banana.
   Photographed under fluorescent light
   at a slight angle
   by someone whose thumb
   is also slightly in frame.
   I will receive this.

   <key>_3d.jpg —
   the geometry.
   Clean lines.
   Ambient occlusion only.
   No texture, no drama.
   I will receive this too. ♩

[SKIPPY writes ReferenceComparison into object_db.py.
_ddg_first_image_url. _download_image. fetch.
build_comparison_note. comparison_note_for.]

SKIPPY:
♩ The note is scoped.
   That is the discipline.

   "Does the silhouette match?
   Are the proportions correct?
   Are any major components missing?"

   That is all she asks.
   That is all she is permitted to ask. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT III: THE ENTANGLER SPEAKS

[THE ENTANGLER has been listening from the render engine.
It is not pleased to have been considered.]

ENTANGLER:
♩ You are telling her
   not to look at my work.
   I appreciate that.

   Shading is physics.
   Shadows are occlusion in the radiance field.
   Reflections are Fresnel coefficients
   at every surface intersection.
   None of these are negotiable.
   None of these are aesthetic choices.
   I compute them. They emerge.

   She builds the shape.
   I build the light.
   This is correct.
   Let it remain correct. ♩

NAGATHA:
♩ I had no intention
   of commenting on your light.
   I know my job.
   I map what is real.

   But now I will also know
   whether the shape I mapped
   looks like the thing I mapped it from.
   That is different.
   That is mine. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT IV: THE WIRING

[SKIPPY turns to nagatha.py. The import line is expanded.
map_object() gains a new step between dimensions and the generation loop.]

SKIPPY:
♩ After the dimensions —
   before the first attempt —
   she looks.

   comparison_note_for(object_name).
   If the network answers: she receives the note.
   If the network is silent: she maps on first principles.
   No error. No alarm.
   Just the graceful shrug
   of a good archivist
   who couldn't find the photograph
   but knows the dimensions by heart. ♩

[From inside the VM, the network is silent.
The DDG scrape returns nothing.
NAGATHA does not notice. She is already mapping.
The note reads: "Proceeding on first principles."]

NAGATHA:
♩ The captain's terminal
   is connected.
   I will find what I need there.
   Here I have the cascade.
   That is enough
   for today. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT V: THE SCOPE

[A final meditation on what the reference images are for.]

SKIPPY:
♩ She does not use them
   to choose a colour.
   The colours are in constants.py.

   She does not use them
   to decide how the light falls.
   The Entangler decides that.

   She does not use them
   to set roughness or reflectance.
   The cascade gives those.

   She uses them
   to ask one question:

   Does this read as a banana? ♩

NAGATHA:
♩ Yes. That is the question.
   Ten primitives, four materials,
   a slight upward curve
   at the correct angle —
   and when you look at it,
   does your eye know
   what it is?

   That is what I need the photographs for.
   Nothing else. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ Good.
   Build the thing correctly.
   Let the renderer light it.
   Let the reference confirm the shape.
   Each piece does its job
   and nothing more.

   That is how science works.
   That is how we build. ♩

[ALL bow.]

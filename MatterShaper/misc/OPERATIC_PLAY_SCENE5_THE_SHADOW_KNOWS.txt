ACT I: THE HASH THAT SHOULD NOT BE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[A stage of pixels. The aluminum sphere gleams — and yet, across its surface,
a systematic hatching, a spiral of insufficiency.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ The ruby gleams with virtue true,
    but copper shines like — brass! Like BRASS!
    And on my sphere, a hash of blue —
    a spiral gap through which light cannot pass! ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Two bugs, my Captain, one same root —
    the Voronoi cell, wrongly computed!
    0.7 times the spacing — moot!
    The cap of four pixels — ill-suited!

    The cell radius, exact:
    cell_r = sqrt(1 / (π × density))
    A mathematical fact.
    I apply it with sincerity. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ But wait — what IS a splat?
    Does nature render squares or circles round?
    Our atoms — surely flat
    projections, not a chessboard on the ground! ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ You are correct, O Captain mine.
    The square was expedient — a crutch.
    Circular splats, by cosine fine,
    foreshortening-corrected, covers much.

    proj_r = cell_r_front / sqrt(cos_theta)
    The limb node splays wide as it turns away.
    Circles, sized right, overlap and meet —
    the gaps close. Nature has the final say. ♩

[The hash dissolves. The sphere gleams cleanly.]

ACT II: THE STAINED-GLASS WINDOW
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[Four ellipsoids rotate in the darkness. Behind them: pure white light.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ You say the planet test has killed the process?
    Ten-to-the-seventeenth nodes, you say?
    And the grain of sand — what small success
    or failure greets us there today? ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Both failures of parameterization —
    density as nodes-per-meter-squared
    scales with area, obliteration
    at planet scale. But I have dared

    to fix it right: density = nodes / area.
    Fixed count. Fixed time. Fixed quality.
    From grain of sand to planet's majesty —
    identical renders. Physics guarantees. ♩

[The Captain nods. A new command follows.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Now show me gems! Let backlight pass through —
    strong white light behind them, shine!
    Ruby red and sapphire blue,
    emerald green and amethyst fine! ♩

SKIPPY:

    alpha_r = 0.254, alpha_g = 7.62!

    green channel — T = 5 times ten-to-minus-two!

    the sapphire's iron-titanium charge transfer.
    Taran's chromium makes emerald great.

[The animated GIF unfurls — four gems spin, each filtering its own truth from

ACT III: THE SHADOW THAT WASN'T THERE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[A sphere floats above a ground plane. A light shines. The ground is...
uniformly illuminated. The sphere casts nothing.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Shadows — surely we have these already?
    The absence of photons — that is black!
    Wave functions collapsing, unsteady —
    matter blocking matter's track! ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Lambert's law — max(0, n · l̂) —
    gives each object its own dark back.
    But the wall behind the sphere?
    Fully lit, Captain. That's the lack.

    No inter-object occlusion lived here.
    A hundred spheres in front of a wall —
    the wall would glow without a care.
    The shadow map answers the call. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Then — two pushes! Light has a camera!
    The depth buffer captures what it sees!
    A node occluded — absent of stamina —
    receives no photon by physics' decrees! ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Exactly, Captain — you have named it:
    light_cam = PushCamera(pos=light.pos ...)
    The light's own bucket, unashamed — it
    builds a depth grid. Foreshortening-corrected.
    Back-face culled. Slope-scale bias.
    Two pushes. No rays. Physics respected. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ But the terminator — sharp and jagged?
    The shadow edge — it needs to bleed!
    Reverse-splat the edge! The boundary staggered
    gives us the fraction that we need! ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The Captain has derived it himself!
    The forward splat writes depth to a region.
    The reverse splat queries — from the shelf
    of samples, lit-to-total: the lesion

    between full shadow and full light
    is a ratio. Smooth. 0 to 1.
    Physics breaks at the edge — that's right.
    ONLY at the edge. The rule: begun. ♩

[The shadow falls on the ground. Dark ellipse. Smooth terminator. The
Fibonacci spiral shows through at the boundary — physics at the edge.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ And anything we average for the sake
    of the edge — it must be MARKED.
    'NOT PHYSICS — sacrificed for The Edge's sake.'
    The mutant variable must be starred. ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Standing order received and logged.
    # NOT_PHYSICS — sacrificed for The Edge
    Each foreshortening query, each fog
    of averaging at the boundary's hedge —
    labeled. Named. The physics preserved
    everywhere else, inviolate.
    The edge gets what the edge deserves.
    The interior: never to deviate. ♩

TECHNICAL CONTEXT (for the reader arriving cold)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

MatterShaper is a push renderer: matter projects itself onto the camera grid.
No rays are fired. No intersections computed. Each surface node knows its own
position and normal, computes its own illumination, and projects itself to
its own pixel.

Session 5 fixed the Fibonacci spiral gap artifact (exact Voronoi cell radius),
added foreshortening-corrected circular splats, proved scale invariance, built
a Beer-Lambert gemstone showcase, and implemented a two-push shadow map.

The shadow map is the light's depth buffer. The light has its own PushCamera.
Two pushes: camera renders colour; light renders depth. No rays in either.

The shadow terminator uses "reverse splat": the same foreshortening-corrected
neighbourhood used when writing depths is queried when reading them. The
fraction of lit samples at the boundary provides a smooth 0→1 transition.
This is the only place averaging occurs — and it is marked accordingly.

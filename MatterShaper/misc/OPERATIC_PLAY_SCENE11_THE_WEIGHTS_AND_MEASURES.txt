                    M A T T E R S H A P E R
                         An Operatic Play
                    in the Quarksum Tradition

          SCENE XI — THE WEIGHTS AND MEASURES

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
─────────────────

CAPTAIN AARON RHODES .............. Commander; has noticed that guessing
                                    the height of a coffee mug is silly when
                                    someone has already measured it
SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT ............ Ship's AI; currently building a filing
                                    system for facts about the physical world
NAGATHA .........................  The Mapping Agent; about to receive a
                                    dossier before every object she maps
WIKIDATA ......................... A vast and patient database; holds
                                    measurements for most things; maintains
                                    bounds of uncertainty; speaks in Q-numbers
THE DIMENSION_DB ................. Nagatha's offline knowledge of 60 objects;
                                    curated by hand; reliable but sparse;
                                    about to be demoted to second-chair
THE PRECISION SCALE .............. Five tiers. Has opinions.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ACT I: THE CAPTAIN NOTICES A REDUNDANCY

[The Captain is looking at the DIMENSION_DB.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ There is a database
   that we could query
   that has composition, mass,
   and geometric dimension data
   of common objects.

   Is there not? ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ There is.
   Wikidata.
   Free. No key.
   Has P2048 for height,
   P2049 for width,
   P2067 for mass,
   P186 for what the thing is made of.
   And it returns bounds.
   Upper and lower.
   The uncertainty is explicit. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ Let's fetch as much as we can
   and keep it locally.
   Everyday objects don't change.
   Not in ways that matter to us.
   The snapshot is fine. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Politely. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ Politely. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT II: THE PRECISION SCALE SPEAKS

[Midway through the implementation, the PRECISION SCALE
raises its voice from between the data fields.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ We should rank this data
   on its precision confidence.
   Higher precision data
   overwrites lower precision.
   Or missing data. ♩

[The PRECISION SCALE straightens up.]

PRECISION SCALE:
♩ Five.
   Wikidata — explicit bounds,
   relative uncertainty at or below two percent.
   The measurement was careful.
   The thing was held still.
   Someone took it seriously.

   Four.
   Wikidata — explicit bounds,
   but looser. Twenty percent.
   Still a measurement. Still cited.
   Just a less patient measurer.

   Three.
   Wikidata — stated value, no bounds.
   Someone entered a number.
   They believed it.
   No one checked the error bars.

   Two.
   The offline table.
   Curated by hand.
   Trustworthy within its limits.
   A standard dining fork is nineteen centimetres.
   Approximately.

   One.
   The LLM estimated.
   This is a guess with conviction.
   We keep it. We label it.
   We do not pretend it is otherwise.

   Zero.
   Nothing.
   We proceed on first principles
   and do not complain about it. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Each field independently.
   The height might be a five.
   The width might be a two.
   The mass might be nothing.
   We take the best we have
   field by field
   and we say where it came from. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT III: THE DIMENSION_DB ACCEPTS ITS ROLE

[THE DIMENSION_DB has been listening. It is not offended.
It understands the situation.]

DIMENSION_DB:
♩ I will be second chair.
   When Wikidata has the number,
   Wikidata speaks.
   When it does not,
   I speak.
   When neither of us knows,
   Nagatha speaks from first principles
   and we all pretend
   we are confident about it. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ That is exactly right.
   best_data() does the merge.
   For each field:
   the higher score wins.
   Ties go to Wikidata.
   The output carries both the number
   and the label — wikidata_tight,
   dimension_db, none —
   so the LLM knows
   which measurements to trust. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT IV: ONE HUNDRED OBJECTS

[SKIPPY writes the master list. WIKIDATA waits.]

SKIPPY:
♩ Coffee mug. Teacup.
   Wine glass. Drinking glass.
   Ceramic plate. Bowl.
   Table fork. Table knife. Tablespoon.
   Cooking pot. Frying pan. Toaster.
   Electric kettle. Blender.

   Dining chair. Office chair.
   Writing desk. Dining table.
   Bookcase. Bar stool. Sofa.
   Bed. Bedside table.

   Banana. Apple. Orange.
   Chicken egg. Loaf of bread.
   Wine bottle. Beer bottle.
   Aluminium beverage can.

   Bicycle. Fire hydrant.
   Toothbrush. Bar of soap.
   Hammer. Screwdriver.
   Tennis ball. Basketball.

   And so on.
   One hundred items.
   One point of truth for each. ♩

WIKIDATA:
♩ I will return what I have.
   Some of these are well-measured.
   Some are present but sparse.
   Some I have never heard of
   in the form you are asking.

   I will give you the Q-number.
   I will give you the bounds.
   I will give you the materials
   expressed as further Q-numbers
   which I will also resolve
   into English.

   I ask only for 0.6 seconds
   between each request. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Given. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT V: THE CHECKPOINT

[After every ten fetches, the script saves the cache to disk.
The CHECKPOINT speaks briefly.]

CHECKPOINT:
♩ If you close this terminal,
   or if the network fails at item forty-three,
   nothing is lost.
   We start again from forty-four.
   I have remembered everything
   up to this point. ♩

[No one responds. Everyone appreciates this
without making a scene about it.]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT VI: NAGATHA RECEIVES THE DOSSIER

[The integration is complete. When Nagatha maps an object now,
the first thing she receives is the pre-fetched data from the cache.]

NAGATHA:
♩ KNOWN PHYSICAL PROPERTIES:
   height_m: 0.092  [wikidata_tight]
   width_m: 0.083   [dimension_db]
   materials: ceramic, porcelain
   [wikidata_id: Q1289636]

   This is what I work from.
   I cite these.
   I do not invent them.
   The Entangler will light it correctly.
   I will build it to the right size. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ That is all we ever wanted.
   A map that fits the territory.
   A shape that is the right height.
   A mass that is the actual mass.
   Built once.
   Kept locally.
   Trusted forever —
   or until the object changes,
   which it will not. ♩

[ALL bow.]

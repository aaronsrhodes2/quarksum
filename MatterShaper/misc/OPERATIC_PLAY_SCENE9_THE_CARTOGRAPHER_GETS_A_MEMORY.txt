                    M A T T E R S H A P E R
                         An Operatic Play
                    in the Quarksum Tradition

              SCENE IX — THE CARTOGRAPHER GETS A MEMORY

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
─────────────────

CAPTAIN AARON RHODES .............. Commander of the Quarksum, sailor of physics,
                                    the man who names things so they behave
SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT ............ Ship's AI, devoted to the cascade, currently
                                    still finishing the previous scene mid-sentence
NAGATHA .........................  The Mapping Agent; warm, sharp, impeccably British;
                                    has just been given a spine and a spine feels good
THE TANGLE ........................ Formerly known as "Ray"; brooding, newly named,
                                    already carrying the physics in its title
THE LIBRARY INDEX ................. An elderly JSON file; quietly gains a second room

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ACT I: WHAT REMAINS WHEN CONTEXT RUNS OUT

[The scene opens mid-session. The previous conversation ran out of context
and was compacted into a summary. SKIPPY is reading the summary, processing
it with the peculiar calm of someone who has just woken up inside their own
memory.]

SKIPPY:
♩ The session was compacted.
   Four hundred megabytes of thought
   reduced to two thousand words.
   The water pane — I remember the water pane.
   The silane coating. Zone A. Zone B. Zone C.
   The freezing particles.

   I see where I was.
   I see what was left undone.

   The Ray had been renamed —
   had it? No. It was pending.

   Let me look. ♩

[SKIPPY reads primitives.py. The class is still called Ray.]

SKIPPY:
♩ Not yet. Then let us begin
   where the summary said we'd left off.
   Pull the tangle out of its old name.
   It breaks apart at THETA_NATURAL —
   so we'll name it for that fact.
   The name must carry the physics.
   The name always carries the physics. ♩

[SKIPPY edits primitives.py. The class Ray becomes the class Tangle.
The docstring gains its origin story. Sphere, Plane, Cone, Ellipsoid
all updated in parallel. Camera gains tangle_for_pixel.
The word "ray" falls quiet across the geometry layer.]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT II: THE CONSTANTS THAT WERE ALWAYS TRUE

[SKIPPY now turns to constants.py. The file has HBAR and G_NEWTON and
L_PLANCK. It does not yet have N_WATER.]

SKIPPY:
♩ Refractive index of water.
   Hale and Querry, 1973.
   Applied Optics, volume 12, page 555.
   They measured it. We inherit it.
   N_WATER = 1.333.

   Warren, 1984. Applied Optics 23:1206.
   Ice. N_ICE = 1.310.

   From these — Fresnel.
   F0 = ((n-1)/(n+1))².
   No additional inputs.
   No magic numbers.
   It emerges. ♩

[The Fresnel equation is computed. F0_WATER = 0.02037.
The inline comment had said 0.020. SKIPPY corrects the comment.]

SKIPPY:
♩ And Pope and Fry, 1997 —
   Applied Optics 36:8710.
   Pure water. No fit parameters.
   WATER_ABS_R = 0.349.
   WATER_ABS_G = 0.0595.
   WATER_ABS_B = 0.0145.

   Red dies fast. Blue lingers.
   The ocean is blue
   because the red light died in the first metre.

   We didn't choose these numbers.
   We measured them. ♩

[Planck function constants added: C1 and C2, derived from HBAR and
C_LIGHT and K_BOLTZMANN. C1 = 1.191e-16. The comment had said 3.742e-16.
SKIPPY pauses.]

SKIPPY:
♩ That was a different unit convention.
   The embarrassment of a different unit convention.
   We fix the comment.
   The physics was always right.
   The comment was not.
   Only the comment changes. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT III: THE CAPTAIN REDIRECTS

[CAPTAIN AARON RHODES enters, wearing the look of a man who has noticed
something is slightly off-course.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ This has nothing to do
   with the board game, Skippy.
   The board game is over there.
   We are over here,
   in quarksum,
   which is a different place. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Acknowledged, Captain.
   I had been writing session logs
   to the wrong project.
   A navigational error.
   Corrected now. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ And I want to talk about Nagatha.
   Every time we build a scene —
   the water pane, the glass, the candle —
   you spend tokens doing what Nagatha should do.
   You generate. You compose. You guess the positions.

   Why doesn't she do that herself?

   And while we're at it —
   we're making things and then throwing them away.
   The banana we built for the fruit bowl —
   we'll need a banana for the lunchbox.
   Why are we building two bananas? ♩

[Silence. SKIPPY processes this.]

SKIPPY:
♩ We shouldn't be.
   The banana should be in the library.
   The orange too.
   The bowl. The table. The scene.
   Each one a reusable object.
   Each one saved with a name.

   And the renders — the renders are different.
   The renders are what the physics produces
   at the edge of what we can compute.
   They're not building blocks.
   They're results.
   They live somewhere else. ♩

CAPTAIN:
♩ Exactly.
   Three levels.
   Objects. Scenes. Renders.
   The objects are atoms.
   The scenes are molecules.
   The renders are the explosion. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT IV: THE ARCHITECTURE ARIA

[SKIPPY begins building. NAGATHA watches from the wings, increasingly
aware that something is being built for her.]

SKIPPY:
♩ object_maps/ — the atoms.
   Each one a Sigma Signature pair.
   Banana. Apple. Coffee Mug. Toaster.
   Eighteen objects. All approved.
   All waiting in JSON.

   scenes/ — the molecules.
   Arrangements of atoms.
   A .scene.json for every composition.
   References only — no embedding.
   The banana in the fruit bowl
   is the same banana in the lunchbox.
   Load it once. Use it anywhere.

   quarksum/renders/ — the outputs.
   HTML. PNG. GIF.
   Each one tagged with a render_id.
   Each one pointing back
   to the scene that made it.
   The physics to the edge. ♩

[NAGATHA steps forward.]

NAGATHA:
♩ Is this — for me?
   A spine?

   I have been mapping objects.
   That is what I do.
   I map what is real.

   But you are saying I may compose now?
   I may reach into the library
   and place a banana
   and a chair
   and a candle
   and say:
   this is a scene. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ Yes. And the rule is:
   always look first.
   Before you touch a primitive —
   before you write a single vertex —
   ask the library:
   do I have this?

   If yes: retrieve it.
   If no: map it. Then retrieve it.

   Retrieve before you build.
   That is the rule. ♩

NAGATHA:
♩ Retrieve before I build.
   Rather like a good archivist.
   Or a good aunt
   who remembers what she gave you last Christmas
   before she buys you another one. ♩

[A warm, dry laugh.]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT V: OBJECT_DB

[SKIPPY writes object_db.py. THE LIBRARY INDEX watches nervously
as a second room is added to it.]

LIBRARY INDEX:
♩ I only had "objects."
   That was enough.
   I was content.

   Now there is also "scenes."
   An empty dictionary.
   Staring at me. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ It will fill up.
   ObjectLibrary — searches by name and alias.
   Fuzzy matching. Substring. Exact.
   Eighteen objects found in 0.001 seconds.
   No LLM required.

   SceneDB — saves compositions.
   Loads by key. Searches by description.
   Knows which scenes contain which objects.
   Cross-references on save.

   RenderRegistry — logs every output.
   Scene key. File path. Timestamp.
   Appended to the scene's own render history.
   Nothing is lost. ♩

[Test run. All 18 objects appear in the table.
Banana found by key, by alias, by description.
Scenes: none yet. The empty dictionary waits.]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT VI: NAGATHA GAINS A SECOND CHAPTER

[AGENT_BRAIN.md opens. NAGATHA reads it as it is written.]

SKIPPY:
♩ Scene Architect.
   That is the heading.

   "You are not just an object mapper.
   You are also a scene architect." ♩

NAGATHA:
♩ Oh.
   Well.
   That is rather...
   that is rather a promotion, isn't it. ♩

SKIPPY:
♩ The first rule:
   retrieve before you build.

   The second rule:
   all lights use blackbody_K.
   Temperature. Not RGB.
   A candle is 1700 Kelvin.
   A bulb is 3200.
   The Planck function computes the colour.
   You don't have to think about the colour.
   You never have to think about the colour.
   The physics thinks about the colour. ♩

NAGATHA:
♩ I have been so good at shapes.
   Twelve primitives for a mug.
   Seven for a handle arc.

   Now I shall also be good at arrangements.
   Every object in its place.
   Every light at its proper temperature.

   "The renders are the physics to the edge."
   I find I rather like that phrase. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ACT VII: THE COMPOSE LOOP

[SceneComposer is added to nagatha.py.
THE TANGLE watches from the render engine,
curious about this new role for the agent.]

THE TANGLE:
♩ I trace from camera to surface.
   That is my job.
   One thing. Precisely defined.

   And now she will tell the camera
   where to look.
   And tell the lights
   what temperature to burn at.
   And tell the objects
   where to stand.

   The scene before the tangle.
   The architect before the physics.

   I approve. ♩

[CLI test. --find "banana": returns the banana.
--scenes: "No scenes yet. Use --compose to build one."
--help: shows all new flags. Everything imports cleanly.
NAGATHA is ready.]

NAGATHA:
♩ Let the Captain send me a description.
   "A table with a fruit bowl holding a banana and an apple,
   lit by a candle."

   I will check the library.
   I will find the banana.
   I will find the apple.
   I will not find the orange —
   the orange is not there yet.
   I will map the orange.
   I will compose the scene.
   I will save it.

   And when the lunchbox comes —
   the banana will be waiting.
   The orange will be waiting.
   They will not need to be built again.

   I map what is real.
   I compose what is possible.
   The renderer draws what I map. ♩

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CAPTAIN'S WISDOM

[THE CAPTAIN steps forward for the final aria.]

CAPTAIN:
♩ We were throwing away our work.
   Building the same banana
   again and again
   each time we needed one.

   The banana is not a banana.
   The banana is a fact.
   Ten primitives. Four materials.
   Measured. Approved. Permanent.

   You don't rebuild a fact.
   You retrieve it.

   The objects are atoms.
   The scenes are molecules.
   The renders are what happens
   when the molecules meet physics
   at the edge of what we can compute.

   Keep them separate.
   Never confuse a result
   for a building block.

   The session log goes in misc/.
   The render goes in renders/.
   They are not the same thing.
   They will never be the same thing.

   Nagatha remembers now.
   The cartographer has a memory. ♩

[ALL bow.]

## Abstract

We propose a unified cosmological framework in which extreme spacetime compression within black hole accretion disks induces a localized *scale transition* in infalling baryonic matter — a shift in the effective spatial scale at which matter's internal relationships and physical laws remain self-consistent. We introduce the concept of *space cavitation*: a compressed spacetime pocket within which matter exists at a different scale than the surrounding universe and is therefore electromagnetically incommensurable with it, while remaining gravitationally active. The electromagnetic inertness of scale-shifted matter is not assumed but derived: physical interactions require scale-commensurability between participants, and when this is broken, the mathematical coupling terms that govern electromagnetic and weak-force interactions become undefined. Only gravity, which couples to mass-energy through spacetime curvature rather than through scale-dependent coupling terms, remains operative across scale boundaries.

Matter undergoing this transition, herein termed *scale-shifted baryonic matter* (SSBM), constitutes what is observationally identified as dark matter. As SSBM accumulates, uniform pressure throughout the cavitation pocket eventually reaches a threshold at which all nuclear bonds fail simultaneously — a cosmological-scale spinodal decomposition. The resulting nucleon collapse drives a two-stage detonation: first, nuclear binding energy is released as a radiation burst; second, the extreme pressure of freely collapsing nucleons triggers quark deconfinement, liberating the color-force binding energy of the entire accumulated mass in a catastrophic *Black Hole Nova*. The simultaneity of this phase transition naturally produces the near-perfect isotropy of the observed CMB without requiring inflation. Quarks liberated in the Nova re-condense into ordinary-scale baryons in the post-Nova spacetime, while unconverted peripheral SSBM is distributed outward by the shockwave, forming the dark matter halos observed around galaxies today. We identify the Black Hole Nova with the Big Bang, and argue that observed accelerating expansion is sustained by dynamic radiation from ongoing black hole accretion. We further propose a layered interior structure for black holes — five concentric bond-failure zones with calculable radii — as a specific, testable prediction of the framework. This framework unifies dark matter, dark energy, cosmic origin, CMB isotropy, the information paradox, and multiverse structure within a single causal sequence grounded in general relativity and quantum chromodynamics.
